James Arthur Announces 2020 North American The YOU Tour Dates

Tickets On Sale To General Public Starting Friday, October 25

Following his arena tour in the UK and EU set for early next year, multi-platinum recording artist James Arthur will embark on his North American ‘The YOU Tour’ with over 25 new shows throughout April and May of 2020. Live Nation is the official promoter of the tour, and singer/songwriter Delacey will support on all dates. 

Fans can keep an eye on James’ social media channels for information on pre-sale tickets, which will become available starting Tuesday, October 22nd at 10am local time. 

Citi is the official presale credit card of the The YOU Tour. As such, Citi cardmembers will have access to purchase presale tickets beginning Tuesday, October 22 at 12PM local time until Thursday, October 24 at 10PM local time through Citi EntertainmentSM. For complete presale details visit www.citientertainment.com.  

Tickets go on sale to the general public beginning Friday, October 25th at 10am local time at LiveNation.com and via James’ official website. James first introduced ‘The YOU Tour’ with a special, limited run of 6 headline shows in September 2019, which all sold out in a matter of minutes. He has also previously toured in the U.S. as an opener for One Republic and Fitz & the Tantrums in 2017.

James has just unveiled his latest full-length album YOU to the world on Friday, October 18 via Columbia Records. The album makes a spectacular statement of intent from one of pop’s most versatile performers. YOU features the retro hip hop of the single “Treehouse” (featuring Ty Dolla $ign and Shotty Horroh), the stately, strings-accompanied track “Quite Miss Home,” the hymnal folk track “Fall,” the soul-rock belter “From Me To You,” the jazzy “Breathe,” and the furious title track “You” which features Travis Barker.  

The last few years have been remarkable for James. He was recently awarded a disc for reaching a billion streams on Spotify and a billion views on YouTube with his No.1 global hit “Say You Won’t Let Go.” Only 18 records have achieved a billion streams on Spotify in history, and James is one of 10 artists globally to do this including Drake, Ed Sheeran and Justin Bieber.
