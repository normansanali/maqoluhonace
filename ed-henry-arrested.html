Ed Henry Arrested, What Happened to Ed Henry ?

Ed Henry arrested in Florida for driving under the influence (DUI).

This incident has sparked discussions about the dangers of impaired driving and has also brought attention to Henry’s previous legal troubles.

Who is Ed Henry?

Ed Henry, also known as Edward Michael Henry Jr., is a respected American journalist with a notable career in the media industry.

He gained fame as the co-host of Fox News Channel’s America’s Newsroom, sharing hosting duties with Sandra Smith. Before joining Fox News, Henry had a successful stint at CNN.

In June 2011, he made the transition to Fox News, assuming the role of White House Correspondent.

However, Henry’s career took a significant downturn in July 2020 when Fox News terminated his employment following an internal investigation into allegations of misconduct.

It’s worth mentioning that Henry vehemently denies these allegations and maintains his innocence. Nevertheless, the investigation’s findings led to his dismissal from the network.

Ed Henry Arrested

The former Fox News anchor Ed Henry was arrested in West Palm, Florida for allegedly driving under the influence.

The arrest occurred after a sheriff’s deputy noticed Henry’s black Cadillac Escalade, which had a flat tire.

During the traffic stop, the deputy observed signs of intoxication such as glassy and bloodshot eyes, an unsteady gait, and slightly slurred speech.

According to reports, Henry admitted to consuming six to seven glasses of bourbon before getting behind the wheel.

Henry also failed the field sobriety tests administered by law enforcement and was taken into custody.

This arrest further compounds the legal challenges that Henry has faced, including previous lawsuits for allegations of misconduct.

Given Henry’s high-profile career as a news anchor, the incident has garnered significant attention.
