Who is Doug Flutie?

Doug Flutie, born on October 23, 1962, in Manchester, Maryland, stands as a prominent figure in American football with a remarkable 21-season career. His enduring legacy is marked by a diverse playing history covering various leagues, including 12 seasons in the NFL, eight in the CFL, and one in the USFL.

Flutie’s crowning achievement came in 1984 when he clinched the Heisman Trophy, solidifying his status as the best college football player of the year. This accolade is a testament to his exceptional skill and impact on the game. Furthermore, his contributions have been enshrined in the College Football Hall of Fame and Canada’s Sports Hall of Fame, underscoring his enduring influence on the sport.

Doug Flutie Career

  • Early Career and College Success (1981-1984):
    • Doug Flutie gained recognition for his famous “Hail Mary” pass against the University of Miami while playing for Boston College.
    • In 1984, he secured the Heisman Trophy, marking him as the best player in college football.
    • During this period, Flutie set the NCAA all-time passing yardage record, a significant milestone for Boston College.
  • Transition to CFL and Grey Cup Victories (1990-1997):
    • Flutie played for the Calgary Stampeders from 1990 to 1996.
    • In 1996, he joined the Toronto Argonauts and led them to consecutive Grey Cup victories in 1996 and 1997.
    • His remarkable 48 touchdown passes in 1994 still stand as a CFL record.
  • Pro Football Milestones and Achievements (1998):
    • In 1998, Doug Flutie achieved a rare feat in pro football, becoming the only quarterback with two seasons of throwing for 6,000 yards.
    • He earned Pro Bowl and NFL Comeback Player of the Year honors while guiding the Buffalo Bills to the playoffs.
